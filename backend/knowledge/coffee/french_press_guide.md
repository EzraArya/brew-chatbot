# French Press Guide

## Overview
The French Press is an immersion brewing method where coffee grounds steep directly in hot water before being separated by a metal mesh filter. It is incredibly simple, requires no paper filters, and produces a rich, full-bodied cup with heavy mouthfeel due to retained coffee oils and micro-fines.

---

## Standard Method

### Best For
- Medium to dark roasts
- Highlighting chocolate, nutty, and earthy notes
- Brewers who want a heavy, textured mouthfeel

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 30g                   |
| Water           | 500g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:16.6                |
| Grind Size      | Coarse                |
| Water Temp      | 93°C / 200°F          |
| Total Brew Time | 4:00 min              |

### Steps
1. Preheat the French Press with hot water, then discard.
2. Add the coarse coffee grounds to the empty carafe.
3. Start the timer and pour all 500g of water vigorously to saturate all grounds.
4. Place the plunger on top (do not press down yet) to retain heat.
5. At 4:00, slowly and gently press the plunger down to the bottom.
6. Pour immediately to prevent over-extraction in the carafe.

### Tips
- Decant any leftover coffee into a thermos immediately; leaving it in the press will continue the extraction and make it bitter.
- Use a burr grinder to minimize "boulders and dust" which cause sludge in the final cup.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Plunging with too much force. This creates channels that push grounds past the mesh screen. Fix by applying gentle, even pressure. If it resists, pull up slightly and push down again.

## James Hoffmann Method (Clean Press)

### Best For
- Light to medium roasts
- Brewers who want the body of a French Press but the clarity of a pour-over
- Eliminating the muddy sludge at the bottom of the cup

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 30g                   |
| Water           | 500g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:16.6                |
| Grind Size      | Medium-Coarse         |
| Water Temp      | 95°C / 203°F          |
| Total Brew Time | 9:00+ min             |

### Steps
1. Add coffee and pour all 500g of water. Do not put the lid on. Let sit for 4 minutes.
2. At 4:00, take a spoon and break the "crust" of coffee at the top, stirring gently.
3. Scoop off the foam and floating bits of coffee with two spoons and discard them.
4. Let the coffee sit undisturbed for another 5-8 minutes so the microscopic fines settle to the bottom.
5. Place the plunger on top but do *not* plunge all the way down. Just press the filter just below the surface of the liquid.
6. Pour slowly and gently through the mesh filter to avoid disturbing the settled grounds at the bottom.

### Tips
- Do not fear the long steep time. Because immersion brewing relies on a diminishing concentration gradient, it is very difficult to over-extract.
- Grinding slightly finer than a standard coarse French press grind works well here because the long settling time mitigates sludge.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Pouring the last few drops into the mug. This transfers the settled silt. Fix by leaving the last ounce of liquid in the carafe.
