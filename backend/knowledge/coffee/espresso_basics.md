# Espresso Basics

## Overview
Espresso is a highly concentrated brewing method where finely ground coffee is tightly packed into a portafilter, and hot water is forced through it at high pressure (typically 9 bars). It forms the foundation of all cafe drinks (lattes, cappuccinos) and requires precise calibration ("dialing in") to achieve balance.

---

## Pulling a Standard Shot

### Best For
- Medium to dark espresso blends
- Straight shots and Americanos
- Balanced extraction of sweetness, acidity, and bitterness

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee (Dose)   | 18g                   |
| Water (Yield)   | 36g                   |
| Ratio           | 1:2                   |
| Grind Size      | Very Fine (Espresso)  |
| Water Temp      | 93°C / 200°F          |
| Total Brew Time | 25–30 seconds         |

### Steps
1. **Purge & Wipe**: Flush the group head with water and thoroughly dry the portafilter basket.
2. **Dose & Distribute**: Grind exactly 18g of coffee into the basket. Use a WDT tool or tap the sides to distribute the grounds evenly.
3. **Tamp**: Press the tamper down perfectly level with firm, even pressure to compact the coffee puck. 
4. **Lock & Pull**: Lock the portafilter into the group head and immediately start the extraction and timer.
5. **Yield**: Stop the shot when your scale reaches 36g of liquid espresso.
6. **Evaluate**: The shot should run like warm honey, starting dark brown and ending in a blond, frothy crema.

### Tips
- Always change only ONE variable at a time when dialing in (start with grind size, leave dose and yield locked).
- Lock the portafilter in gently; slamming it can fracture the puck, leading to channeling.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Uneven tamping. If the tamp is slanted, water will rush to the thinner side of the puck, over-extracting one half and under-extracting the other. Fix by holding the tamper level using your thumb and index finger against the edge of the basket.

## Dialing In (Troubleshooting)

### Best For
- Finding the sweet spot for a new bag of coffee
- Adjusting extraction daily as beans age

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Choked Shot     | >35 seconds           |
| Fast Shot       | <20 seconds           |
| Pump Pressure   | 9 Bars                |
| Dose Variance   | +/- 0.1g              |
| Temp Variance   | +/- 1°C               |
| Crema Color     | Hazelnut / Tiger-stripe|

### Steps
1. Pull a baseline shot strictly measuring 18g in, 36g out. Note the time.
2. Taste the shot. If it's intensely sour and pulled in 15 seconds, the water flowed too fast.
3. Adjust the grinder to be slightly *finer* to add resistance. Purge 2g of coffee to clear the old grind from the chute.
4. Pull again. If it is terribly bitter, dry, and pulled in 40 seconds, the water flowed too slowly.
5. Adjust the grinder to be slightly *coarser*.
6. Repeat until the 1:2 ratio pulls in 25-30 seconds and tastes balanced.

### Tips
- "Dialing in" is a daily task. As beans release CO2 (age) over the week, they offer less resistance. You will need to grind slightly finer as the bag gets older.
- A bottomless portafilter is the best diagnostic tool; it allows you to visibly see channeling or uneven extraction (spraying).

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Ignoring the grinder purge. If you change the grind setting but don't purge a few grams, your next shot will be a mix of the old and new grind sizes, throwing off your calibration.

## Quick Reference

| Variable | Adjustment | Result on Flow | Result on Taste |
|----------|------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Grind    | Finer      | Slower         | More bitter, less sour |
| Grind    | Coarser    | Faster         | More sour, less bitter |
| Dose     | Increase   | Slower         | Higher intensity |
| Ratio    | Increase (1:2.5)| Faster    | Diluted intensity, tamed acidity |
