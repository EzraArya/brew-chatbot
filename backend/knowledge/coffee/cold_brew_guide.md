# Cold Brew Guide

## Overview
Cold brew is an immersion method that extracts coffee using cold or room-temperature water over a prolonged period (12-24 hours). The lack of heat means certain acidic and bitter compounds are never extracted, resulting in a remarkably smooth, sweet, and low-acidity beverage with high caffeine content.

---

## Cold Brew Concentrate

### Best For
- Batch prepping for the week
- Mixing with milk, syrups, or pouring over ice without dilution
- Maximizing fridge space

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 100g                  |
| Water           | 400g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:4                   |
| Grind Size      | Extra Coarse          |
| Water Temp      | Cold / Room Temp      |
| Total Brew Time | 16–24 hours           |

### Steps
1. Grind the coffee as coarse as your grinder allows (like breadcrumbs).
2. Place coffee in a large mason jar, French press, or dedicated cold brew bag.
3. Pour filtered, cold water over the grounds, ensuring all coffee is saturated. Gently stir once.
4. Cover the container and leave it on the counter for 16-24 hours (or in the fridge for 24+ hours).
5. Filter the coffee. If using a jar, strain through a fine mesh sieve first, then through a paper coffee filter to remove all silt.
6. Store the concentrate in an airtight vessel in the fridge.

### Tips
- Because this is a 1:4 concentrate, do NOT drink it straight. Dilute it 1:1 or 1:2 with water or milk before serving.
- Coffee beans that are slightly past their prime (older than 4 weeks) are perfect for cold brew, as the cold extraction hides the staleness.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Using a fine grind. Fine grinds will over-extract during the long steep time, leading to muddy, bitter cold brew, and they will completely clog your paper filter during decanting.

## Ready-to-Drink (RTD) Method

### Best For
- Drinking black straight from the fridge
- Brewers who want to avoid the tedious double-filtering process of thick concentrates
- Highlighting delicate single-origin flavors

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 50g                   |
| Water           | 600g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:12                  |
| Grind Size      | Coarse                |
| Water Temp      | Cold / Room Temp      |
| Total Brew Time | 12–16 hours           |

### Steps
1. Add coarsely ground coffee to your brewing vessel.
2. Pour in 600g of filtered water and stir well to saturate.
3. Let sit at room temperature for 12 to 16 hours.
4. Strain the coffee using a metal filter or paper filter. Since the liquid is less viscous than concentrate, it will filter much faster.
5. Serve immediately over ice, no dilution needed. 

### Tips
- Light roasts shine in the RTD method, producing a bright, refreshing, and floral iced coffee that gets lost in heavy concentrates.
- Adjust the steep time based on temperature: warmer rooms extract faster (12h), fridges extract slower (20h).

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Steeping too long at room temperature. After 24 hours at room temp, cold brew can begin to taste woody, astringent, and stale. Keep it under 18 hours if not refrigerated.
