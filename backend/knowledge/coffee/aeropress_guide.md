# AeroPress Guide

## Overview
The AeroPress is a versatile, pressure-assisted immersion brewer. It utilizes a plastic syringe-like chamber to steep coffee before forcing it through a small paper filter under manual pressure. It is highly portable, forgiving, and capable of brewing everything from espresso-like concentrates to clean, filter-style cups.

---

## Standard Method

### Best For
- Medium to dark roasts
- A quick, clean, and highly repeatable daily cup
- Highlighting sweetness and body

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 15g                   |
| Water           | 250g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:16.7                |
| Grind Size      | Medium-Fine           |
| Water Temp      | 85°C / 185°F          |
| Total Brew Time | 2:00 min              |

### Steps
1. Place a paper filter in the cap, rinse with hot water, and lock it onto the brew chamber.
2. Place the chamber on a sturdy mug and add the coffee grounds.
3. Start the timer and pour 250g of water, ensuring all grounds are saturated.
4. Stir gently back and forth 3-4 times.
5. Insert the plunger about half an inch into the top of the chamber and pull up slightly to create a vacuum (this stops coffee from dripping through). Wait until 1:30.
6. Slowly press the plunger down over 30 seconds. Stop when you hear a hissing sound.

### Tips
- Use lower temperature water (around 85°C) if you want to reduce bitterness, a hallmark of the AeroPress inventor's original recipe.
- Clean up is instant: remove the cap, push the plunger entirely through, and pop the "puck" of grounds into the compost.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Pressing too hard. High pressure causes channeling and uneven extraction. Fix by resting the weight of your forearms on the plunger; let gravity do the work over 30 seconds.

## Inverted Method

### Best For
- Light roasts requiring longer steeps
- Full immersion control without premature dripping
- Concentrated "faux-espresso" bases for milk drinks

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 18g                   |
| Water           | 200g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:11                  |
| Grind Size      | Fine                  |
| Water Temp      | 93°C / 200°F          |
| Total Brew Time | 2:30 min              |

### Steps
1. Push the plunger about 1 inch into the brew chamber. Stand the AeroPress upside down on the plunger (the open hexagonal end should face up).
2. Add coffee into the open chamber.
3. Pour water up to the top (approx 200g). Stir vigorously.
4. Let steep until 2:00.
5. Rinse a filter, place it in the cap, and screw the cap tightly onto the chamber.
6. Place a mug upside down over the cap. Carefully and swiftly flip the entire assembly (mug + AeroPress) right-side up.
7. Plunge slowly for 30 seconds until the hiss. Add hot water to the mug to bypass if desired.

### Tips
- Ensure the plunger seal is perfectly dry before setting it up inverted. A wet seal can slip, causing a dangerous spill of boiling water.
- This concentrated ratio (1:11) is perfect for pouring over ice or mixing with steamed milk.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: The flip disaster. Failing to hold the plunger and chamber securely during the flip can cause the chamber to detach. Fix by gripping both the main chamber barrel and the mug firmly when inverting.
