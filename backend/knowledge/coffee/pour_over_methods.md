# Pour Over Methods

## Overview
Pour over is a manual drip brewing method that involves pouring hot water over coffee grounds in a filter. It offers unparalleled control over water temperature, pour speed, and extraction, making it the preferred method for highlighting the delicate, complex flavor notes in specialty coffee.

---

## Hario V60

### Best For
- Light to medium roasts
- Highlighting floral, fruity, and acidic notes
- Brewers who want precise control over flow rate

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 15g                   |
| Water           | 250g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:16.7                |
| Grind Size      | Medium-Fine           |
| Water Temp      | 93°C / 200°F          |
| Total Brew Time | 2:30–3:00 min         |

### Steps
1. Fold the filter seam, place in V60, and rinse with hot water. Discard rinse water.
2. Add coffee grounds and tap the brewer to level the bed. Create a small well in the center.
3. Start timer and pour 45g of water in a spiral motion to bloom (wait until 0:45).
4. Pour in concentric circles up to 150g, maintaining a steady flow. Wait for the water to drain slightly (until 1:15).
5. Pour the remaining water up to 250g. Give the brewer a gentle swirl to flatten the bed.
6. Allow the water to draw down completely. The bed should be flat.

### Tips
- Use a gooseneck kettle; precision pouring is critical for the V60 due to its large center hole.
- If the drawdown is too fast, swirl the bloom slightly to increase agitation.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Pouring directly on the paper filter. Water bypasses the coffee, leading to a weak, watery cup. Fix by keeping pours at least 1cm away from the edges.

## Chemex

### Best For
- Light roasts
- Producing an exceptionally clean, tea-like body
- Brewing larger batches (3+ cups)

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 30g                   |
| Water           | 500g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:16.7                |
| Grind Size      | Medium-Coarse         |
| Water Temp      | 95°C / 203°F          |
| Total Brew Time | 3:30–4:30 min         |

### Steps
1. Place the thick Chemex filter in the carafe with the 3-ply side facing the spout. Rinse thoroughly and discard water.
2. Add coffee and level the bed.
3. Pour 90g of water for the bloom. Gently stir with a spoon to ensure all grounds are wet (wait until 0:45).
4. Slowly pour in spirals up to 300g, keeping the water level high. Let it drain slightly (until 1:45).
5. Pour the remaining water up to 500g.
6. Wait for the final drawdown and remove the filter.

### Tips
- The Chemex filter is 20-30% heavier than standard filters, which removes most coffee oils. Rinse it with extra hot water to remove any papery taste.
- Grind coarser than you would for a V60 to prevent the thick filter from stalling the brew.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Choked filter/stalled brew. The filter seals against the spout, creating a vacuum. Fix by ensuring the 3-ply side rests against the spout to keep the air channel open.

## Kalita Wave

### Best For
- Medium to dark roasts
- Consistently balanced, sweeter, and fuller-bodied cups
- Beginners (forgiving flat-bottom design)

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Coffee          | 20g                   |
| Water           | 330g                  |
| Ratio           | 1:16.5                |
| Grind Size      | Medium                |
| Water Temp      | 92°C / 198°F          |
| Total Brew Time | 3:00–3:30 min         |

### Steps
1. Place the fluted filter in the Kalita. Rinse very gently by pouring water directly into the center to avoid collapsing the ridges. Discard water.
2. Add coffee and shake gently to level the flat bed.
3. Pour 60g of water to bloom (wait until 0:45).
4. Pour in slow, continuous spirals up to 200g (until 1:30).
5. Pulse pour the remaining 130g in small batches, maintaining a consistent water level until complete.
6. Let the coffee draw down completely.

### Tips
- Pulse pouring works best here to maintain the temperature mass in the flat bottom bed.
- The 3 small holes restrict flow, so your pour technique doesn't need to be as aggressively controlled as the V60.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Flattening the filter ridges during rinsing. This ruins the airflow and stalls the brew. Fix by pouring rinse water dead center, never on the walls.
