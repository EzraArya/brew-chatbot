# Water Chemistry

## Overview
Coffee is 98% water. The minerals dissolved in your water act as the chemical "extractors" that pull flavor compounds out of the roasted bean. Water that is too soft will leave your coffee tasting flat and acidic, while water that is too hard will mute the acidity and make the coffee taste chalky or dull.

---

## Ideal Water Profile

### Best For
- Specialty coffee brewing (pour-over and espresso)
- Protecting expensive espresso machines from scale buildup
- Extracting vibrant, clean flavors

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| TDS (Total)     | 75 - 150 mg/L (ppm)   |
| Calcium Hardness| 50 - 68 mg/L          |
| Alkalinity      | 40 - 50 mg/L          |
| pH              | 6.5 - 7.5 (Neutral)   |
| Sodium          | < 10 mg/L             |
| Chlorine        | 0 mg/L                |

### Steps
1. Test your tap water using a TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) meter and aquarium drop-test kits (for GH and KH).
2. If your tap water has chlorine, filter it through a carbon filter (like a Brita) before using.
3. If your tap water TDS is above 200ppm, it is too hard. Cut it with distilled water to bring the TDS down.
4. If your tap water is below 50ppm (too soft), it lacks the minerals to extract flavor. You must add minerals back in.
5. Heat the water to the proper brewing temperature. Mineral solubility changes with heat, but the chemical makeup remains the baseline for extraction.

### Tips
- **Calcium and Magnesium** are the heavy lifters. Magnesium extracts fruity/floral notes, while Calcium extracts heavier, chocolaty notes.
- **Alkalinity (Bicarbonate)** acts as a buffer. Too much buffer neutralizes the pleasant acidity in coffee (flat taste). Too little buffer makes the coffee unpleasantly sour.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Using 100% pure Distilled or Reverse Osmosis (RO) water to brew coffee. Without minerals, pure water is highly reactive; it over-extracts bitter compounds and tastes incredibly hollow. Furthermore, pure RO water will corrode the metal boilers inside espresso machines over time.

## Custom Mineral Water (Zero-Water Method)

### Best For
- Absolute consistency in every brew
- People living in areas with extreme hard/soft tap water
- Advanced brewers looking to tweak specific flavor profiles

### Parameters
| Parameter       | Recommended Value     |
|-----------------|-----------------------|
| Base Water      | 1 Gallon Distilled    |
| Epsom Salt      | 1.45g (Magnesium)     |
| Baking Soda     | 0.45g (Alkalinity)    |
| Calcium Chloride| Optional (Body)       |
| Shaking Time    | 30 seconds            |
| Cost per Gal    | < $0.50               |

### Steps
1. Purchase a one-gallon jug of distilled or Reverse Osmosis water.
2. Measure 1.45 grams of Epsom Salt (Magnesium Sulfate) using a highly accurate jewelry scale.
3. Measure 0.45 grams of Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate).
4. Add the minerals directly into the gallon jug.
5. Cap the jug and shake vigorously for 30-60 seconds until all powders are completely dissolved.
6. Use this engineered water for all your coffee brewing.

### Tips
- This specific recipe is known as the "Barista Hustle Water Recipe" and is a fantastic, cheap baseline that hits SCA (Specialty Coffee Association) standards perfectly.
- You can buy pre-mixed mineral packets (like Third Wave Water) to dump into distilled water if you don't want to measure raw chemicals.

### Common Mistakes
- **Mistake**: Confusing Baking Powder with Baking Soda. Baking powder contains cornstarch and acids. Fix by ensuring you are using 100% pure Sodium Bicarbonate (Baking Soda) and 100% pure Magnesium Sulfate (Epsom Salt without scents/dyes).

## Quick Reference

| Mineral Agent | Role in Extraction | Too High Result | Too Low Result |
|---------------|--------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Magnesium/Calcium | Flavor extraction | Chalky, muted acidity | Weak, hollow body |
| Bicarbonate (Buffer)| Acidity control | Flat, boring, dull | Aggressively sour |
| Chlorine      | Sanitation         | Chemical/medicinal taste | N/A |
